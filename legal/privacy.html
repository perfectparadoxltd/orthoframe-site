The Short Version

OrthoFrame runs entirely on your Mac. Your patient photos, your library, and everything you do inside the app never leave your computer. The AI that classifies photos runs on-device. We have no servers that receive your data or your patients' data, no analytics SDK, no telemetry, no tracking.

Because your patients' photos never leave your Mac and we never receive them, you (the dentist or clinic) are the data controller for your patients' data — not us. OrthoFrame is a local tool that helps you keep that data safe.

The only personal data we (or our payment partner, Paddle) handle is what is needed to sell you a one-time licence and answer your support emails. That's the whole story — the rest of this document spells it out.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how we handle personal data in connection with OrthoFrame (the "App"), our website https://perfectparadox.co.uk, and one-time licence sales.

OrthoFrame is a native macOS application (macOS 14 and later) for organising clinical orthodontic photography — sorting patient photos into ABO record sheets. It is not a medical device. It performs no diagnosis, no measurement, and makes no clinical decision.

This policy is issued in compliance with:

  • The UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • The Data Protection Act 2018
  • The Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR)
  • The EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR), where applicable to EU-based users

For the limited personal data described in Section 4, Perfect Paradox Ltd is the data controller. For your purchase transaction, Paddle acts as the Merchant of Record and is an independent data controller for the payment data it collects (see Section 5).

For the patient photos and clinical data you process inside the App, see Section 3 — in short, you are the controller and we are neither a controller nor a cloud processor, because that data never leaves your device and we never receive it.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

This is one of the most important sections of this policy. The App does not collect, access, transmit, store, or process on any server of ours:

  • Your patient photos or your photo library — every image you import, sort, classify, annotate, or export stays on your Mac. The on-device AI classifier never uploads photos anywhere.
  • Patient identities or clinical records — patient names, record sheets, and case data live only in your local library.
  • Your content or activity — what you do in the App is not logged, measured, or reported to anyone.
  • Usage analytics or telemetry — the App contains no analytics SDK, no tracking pixels, no crash-reporting service that sends data automatically, and no phone-home functionality beyond what is disclosed in Section 6.
  • Personal identifiers from your Mac — no advertising IDs, no fingerprinting, no contact lists, no location.

This is verifiable: you can monitor the App's network activity with any firewall tool (e.g., Little Snitch or LuLu) and confirm that the only connection it makes is the one listed in Section 6.

3. Patient Data and the Controller / Processor Split

This is the section that matters most for a clinical tool. Please read it carefully.

The photos OrthoFrame helps you organise are your patients' personal data, and clinical facial and intraoral photography is sensitive, health-adjacent information that attracts a high duty of care under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 (and the EU GDPR where it applies).

  • You are the data controller. Because patient photos never leave your Mac and we never receive, see, or store them, the dentist or clinic using OrthoFrame is the data controller for their patients' data. You are responsible for your own obligations to your patients — including establishing a lawful basis and appropriate consent for taking and keeping clinical photographs, your retention schedule, patient rights requests, and the physical and digital security of the Mac on which the library is stored.
  • We are not a cloud processor. OrthoFrame is a local tool that runs on your machine. There is no transmission of patient data to us, so we do not act as a processor handling your patients' data on a server on your behalf. We cannot access, retrieve, or delete your patient library — only you can.
  • No data-processing agreement with us is required. Because we never receive or process your patients' data, no Article 28 UK GDPR data-processing agreement between you and us is required for the patient data handled inside OrthoFrame; you remain the sole controller.

OrthoFrame includes features designed to help you meet your obligations as controller:

  • EXIF / GPS metadata stripping on import — when you import photos, embedded metadata (including camera GPS location, device identifiers, and timestamps) is removed, so that information is not carried into your library.
  • Optional eye-bar anonymisation — on export, print, or share you may apply an eye-bar to de-identify faces. This is applied to the exported/printed/shared copy only; the originals stored in your library retain the full, un-redacted face so your clinical record remains complete.
  • Optional encrypted backup (Pro) — the Pro tier can produce an AES-256-GCM encrypted backup of your library so that off-device copies are protected at rest.

We strongly recommend you also enable FileVault on your Mac and keep regular, secure backups, as the security of your patients' data ultimately depends on the security of the machine it lives on.

6. Network Connections the App Makes

Your licence is validated entirely on your Mac. The App verifies your licence key offline using a cryptographic signature (Ed25519, with the public key embedded in the App) — it does not contact us, Paddle, or any server to activate or check your licence, and it transmits nothing when you enter your key. Your licence is perpetual and never expires; entering a valid key permanently unlocks your tier on the Macs you own or control.

In normal use, OrthoFrame makes no network connections at all. The only connection it can ever make is optional and user-initiated:

  • Manual update check (optional): automatic update checks are switched off by default. If — and only if — you choose to check for updates from within the App, it contacts our update server to see whether a newer version exists. This transmits the App version, your macOS version, and basic CPU type — no personal data, no licence key, no patient data, and no identifier used for tracking.

No other connections are made, ever. Your patient photos and library never require a network connection and are never transmitted anywhere. You can confirm all of this with a firewall tool such as Little Snitch or LuLu.
