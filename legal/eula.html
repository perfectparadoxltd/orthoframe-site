2. What OrthoFrame Is — and Is Not

OrthoFrame is a native macOS productivity tool that helps orthodontic and dental professionals organise clinical photography — sorting patient photographs into ABO-style record sheets and managing a local photo library.

OrthoFrame is not a medical device. It performs no diagnosis, no measurement, no cephalometric or clinical analysis, and makes no treatment recommendations. Its on-device AI classifies and sorts photographs into views; it does not interpret them clinically. All clinical judgement, diagnosis, and treatment decisions remain entirely the responsibility of the treating clinician. See Section 9.

5. Privacy, Local Operation, and Patient Data

5.1 Everything Stays on Your Mac

The Software runs entirely locally on your Mac. Patient photographs, the library, and all activity never leave your computer. The AI photo-classification runs on-device. Perfect Paradox operates no server that receives your data or your patients' data; there is no analytics SDK, no telemetry, and no tracking of any kind. This can be independently verified with an outbound-firewall tool such as Little Snitch or LuLu.

5.2 Network Connections the Software Makes

Your licence is validated entirely offline on your Mac using a cryptographic signature; the Software does not contact us, our payment provider, or any server to activate or check your licence, and transmits nothing when you enter your key. In normal use the Software makes no outbound network connection at all. The only connection it can ever make is optional and user-initiated: if you choose to check for updates, it contacts our update server for version information only (App version, macOS version, CPU type) — never your licence key, personal data, or patient data. Automatic update checks are disabled by default. The Software is fully usable offline.

5.3 You Are the Data Controller for Patient Data

The photographs the Software handles are your patients' personal data. Clinical facial and intraoral photographs taken by a dental professional are likely to constitute special-category health data under Article 9 of the UK GDPR and EU GDPR, and their processing therefore requires an Article 9 condition (for example, the provision of healthcare or the patient's explicit consent) in addition to an Article 6 lawful basis. Because that data never leaves your Mac and we never receive it, you (the dentist or clinic) are the data controller for your patients' data. You are responsible for your own obligations to your patients under UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and/or EU GDPR — including establishing both an Article 6 lawful basis and an Article 9 condition, obtaining any necessary consent, applying appropriate retention, and securing your Mac.

Because patient data is never transmitted to us, OrthoFrame is a local tool you operate yourself; it is not a cloud processor handling patient data on your behalf. We never receive, store, or have any access to your patients' photographs.

5.4 How OrthoFrame Helps You Meet Your Obligations

The Software includes features designed to support your data-protection responsibilities:

  • EXIF/GPS stripping on import — location and other embedded metadata are removed from photographs as they are imported.
  • Optional eye-bar anonymisation on export, print, or share. Your stored originals retain the full face; the anonymisation applies only to the copies you choose to export, print, or share.
  • Optional AES-256-GCM encrypted library backup (Pro tier).

We strongly recommend that you also enable FileVault full-disk encryption on your Mac and maintain your own regular backups of your library.
