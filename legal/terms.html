2. What OrthoFrame Is (and Isn't)

OrthoFrame is a native macOS application for organising clinical orthodontic photography — sorting patient photos into ABO record sheets. It runs entirely on your Mac and requires macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later.

  • It is not a medical device. It performs no diagnosis, measurement, or clinical assessment of any kind. It is an organisational tool. All clinical judgement, diagnosis, and treatment decisions remain entirely yours as the treating clinician.
  • It is not a cloud service. There is no account on our servers holding your data. Your patient photos, your library, and all activity within the App stay on your Mac and never leave the computer. The AI photo-classification that sorts images into record sheets runs entirely on-device. We operate no server that receives your or your patients' data, and there is no analytics SDK, no telemetry, and no tracking in the App. You can verify this yourself with a firewall tool such as Little Snitch or LuLu.
  • Its licence is validated entirely offline on your Mac using a cryptographic signature — the App contacts no server to activate or check your licence, and transmits nothing when you enter your key. In normal use the App makes no network connection at all; the only connection it can ever make is an optional, user-initiated update check (version information only — no licence key, personal data, or patient data). Automatic update checks are disabled by default. See the Privacy Policy for details.

3.2 How OrthoFrame Helps You Meet Those Obligations

OrthoFrame includes features designed to support your privacy and data-protection duties:

  • Optional AES-256-GCM encrypted library backup (Pro tier).

We recommend you enable macOS FileVault and maintain your own backups. The security of your Mac and your patient records is your responsibility.
